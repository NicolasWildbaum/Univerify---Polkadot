Univerify

Federated Academic Credential Registry on Polkadot

"Hi — I want to start with a problem everyone in this room will eventually face: verifying that someone actually has the degree they claim to have."

Motivation & The Problem

Institution Dependency

Verification means calling the university — slow, opaque, and fails when the institution closes or loses records.

No Cryptographic Proof

PDFs carry no verifiable link to the issuer. Any diploma can be forged in minutes with basic software.

Who is the authority — and can we build a system that works without one?

"[Open with personal story — ~20 seconds.] The point is structural, not just inconvenient. Any single authority is a failure mode — the university closes, the admin key leaks, the company shuts down. The question is: can we replace the central authority with a protocol?"

A Web3 Approach

Credential

keccak256(canonical claims) — a hash, not a PDF. No document on-chain.

Privacy

No PII on-chain. Holder name never touches the chain — only the hash.

Governance

Active universities govern admission and removal. No owner. No admin key.

Ownership

Soulbound ERC-721 minted atomically — non-transferable, mirrors revocation.

Runtime

Solidity contracts on Polkadot via pallet-revive — EVM execution, Substrate wallets.

Verification

Verifier recomputes the hash locally and calls the chain — no middleman, no API, no permission needed.

"Core insight: you don't verify a document, you verify a hash against a registry. The PII constraint is deliberate — even a fully compromised chain reveals nothing about holders. 'No owner' is not a gap — it's the design: every write privilege flows from Active-issuer status. pallet-revive bridges EVM and Substrate: same Solidity ABI, Polkadot security and wallets. On 'trustless, permissionless, public' — three distinct properties: — Trustless: the outcome is determined purely by math, not by trusting any institution. The verifier recomputes the hash client-side and calls verifyCertificate on the chain. If the hashes match and the cert is not revoked, it is valid — full stop. No need to trust Univerify, the issuing university, or any intermediary service. — Permissionless: anyone — an employer, a government office, a random person — can verify any credential at any time. No account, no fee, no whitelist, no API key. The contract is public. — Public: the contract source and state live on a public blockchain. The verification logic can be audited, forked, or replicated by anyone, and it will remain queryable as long as the chain runs — no company needs to stay in business for it to work."

"At a high level, Univerify has a web app used by universities, students, and verifiers. Reads go through viem, writes go through PAPI, and both interact with the on-chain registry. The registry stores the source of truth, the NFT contract represents certificate ownership, and PDFs or metadata remain off-chain as verifiable evidence."

Governance

stateDiagram-v2 direction LR [*] --> None None --> Pending : applyAsIssuer Pending --> Active : threshold approvals Active --> Removed : threshold removal votes Removed --> Pending : re-apply new epoch

No Owner. No Admin Key.

Every write privilege flows from Active-issuer status — no back door.

Symmetric Trust

Same approvalThreshold governs both admission and removal.

"No owner, no admin key. The only actors are Active issuers. Symmetric threshold: the quorum that admits you is the same quorum that removes you. issuerEpoch trick: approvals keyed by (candidate, epoch, approver) — increment epoch, old records are unreachable without touching them. O(1) invalidation, no mass writes. Known trade-off: if active count drops below threshold, system deadlocks. Recovery = redeploy. Accepted to keep the model simple."

Limitations & Future Improvements

Wallet compatibility

PWallet didn't work. Bulletin and Talisman had integration issues. The demo runs on dev accounts.

UX

One-click account setup, guided issue → verify → share flow, QR deep-link for mobile credential sharing.

Storage

Auto-pin to IPFS on issuance; CID stored on-chain as the canonical retrieval reference.

Selective disclosure

ZK circuit over claimsHash — prove "I hold a degree" without revealing institution, name, or date.

"Wallet: we tried PWallet — didn't work. Bulletin and Talisman both had integration issues with pallet-revive on Paseo. The demo uses dev accounts (Alice, Bob, Charlie). This is the #1 thing that needs to be fixed before any real user can touch the product. UX: the goal is to make the crypto layer completely invisible. One wallet click, done. Storage: auto-pin to IPFS on issuance so the document survives beyond the chain. ZK selective disclosure: prove 'I hold a degree' without revealing institution, name, or date. Requires a ZK circuit that takes claims as private inputs and outputs a proof verifiable against the on-chain claimsHash."

Beyond Univerify

Univerify is just the beginning. The same model applies to any institution that makes claims about people.

Medical Licences

Verify a doctor's qualification — anywhere in the world, instantly.

Employment Records

An employer attests work history — portable, verifiable, no HR middleman.

DAO Membership

Gate access to on-chain communities based on verifiable real-world credentials.

Undercollateralised Loans

A DeFi protocol lends against a verified reputation instead of locked capital.

"Univerify proves the model works for universities. But the same infrastructure — federated governance, hash-based claims, soulbound NFTs — generalises to any issuing institution. Think about what becomes possible: Medical licensing boards could issue on-chain licences. A hospital in another country could verify a doctor's credentials in milliseconds without making a phone call. Employment records issued by employers — a portable work history you own, not LinkedIn. Professional bodies like bar associations or engineering institutes could issue membership credentials that gate access to on-chain services. DAO membership: instead of token-gating, gate by verified real-world qualification. DeFi: undercollateralized lending based on verified reputation — a student loan backed by a verified degree, not collateral the student doesn't have. And there's a natural connection to proof-of-personhood systems like Proof of Humanity or World ID. Univerify proves *what you achieved*; personhood proves *you are a unique human*. Combined, you get a complete identity layer: who you are and what you've done — without a central authority holding either."
